What is a bug?

Bugs are six legged creatures also known as insects. Insects make up a lagre majority of all the moving creatures on the earth.

Fun Facts

The largest beetle known is the Goliath Beetle.
The largest butterfly known is the Queen Alexandra's Birdwing, a very rare butterfly that was first mistaken for a bird.

ARTICLES

Article 1: Why are bugs important?

Bugs help us in many ways. Mantids eat those pesky flies that land on your food. There would be much less fruit in the world without bees and butterflies pollinating. Bugs do things that greatly help us. Without bugs, we would not have that many reptiles, and the things that eat them. Without bugs, even we might not be here


Article 2: Save a bug!

It is important to save bugs. Think of the Xerxes, the brilliant bluish-purple butterfly that went extinct. Can we bring it back? No. Then why are we killing them in the first place? If we can't fix it, then why do we break it? Start saving bugs. You can save them by following the tips below.
